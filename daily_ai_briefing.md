# Morning AI Dashboard – April 2024

---

## Top 3 News

1. **Meta Launches Llama 3 & Expands AI Ecosystem**
   - Meta released Llama 3, a state-of-the-art open-source large language model available in 8B and 70B parameter sizes. It delivers superior performance versus Llama 2 and key rivals, and crucially, is free for developers via API without fees.  
   - Integration begins across flagship products: WhatsApp leverages Llama 3 for physically guided tasks using camera inputs, while Ray-Ban AI glasses pilot new real-world assistant features.
   - Meta’s aggressive AI push includes rolling out Meta AI services across Facebook, Instagram, WhatsApp, and Messenger in 12+ countries, bolstering image generation and assistant capabilities.
   - *Business Impact:* Meta’s free, powerful open model approach challenges dominant players’ paywalled offerings, aiming to reclaim developer mindshare and embed LLM tech deeply into everyday consumer experiences.  
   [Source: University of Notre Dame, LinkedIn, Everypixel]

2. **Microsoft & Coca-Cola Ink $1.1 Billion AI Cloud Partnership**
   - A major five-year deal to accelerate Coca-Cola’s digital transformation via Microsoft Azure cloud and generative AI initiatives focused on marketing, supply chain optimization, and manufacturing improvement.
   - Microsoft further strengthens AI talent by hiring DeepMind co-founder to lead consumer AI efforts, signaling a strategic leadership pivot.
   - This deal exemplifies growing enterprise investment in integrating GenAI into core business operations to drive efficiency and competitive advantage.
   - *Business Impact:* Microsoft’s expanded partnership pipeline and leadership hires solidify Azure’s position as a leading AI cloud platform, driving commercial adoption at scale.  
   [Source: Everypixel, Zeta Alpha]

3. **Global AI Safety & Regulation Cooperative Moves**
   - The US and UK deepen collaboration through a Memorandum of Understanding covering joint AI safety initiatives, personnel exchanges, and aligned testing on public AI models—a landmark in international AI governance cooperation.
   - Concurrently, the US federal government appoints Chief AI Officers across agencies including Defense and Intelligence to oversee AI strategy execution.
   - These developments highlight accelerating government efforts to impose oversight frameworks ensuring responsible AI deployment amid rapid technological advancement.
   - *Business Impact:* Companies must prepare for tighter compliance regimes and opportunity to engage proactively in shaping AI policy and standards globally.  
   [Source: University of Notre Dame]

## Global Pulse

- **International AI Governance Strengthens:**  
  The US-UK MOU exemplifies expanding cross-national cooperation on AI safety and standards, establishing a precedent for more international AI oversight frameworks.

- **Federal Government Push on AI Leadership:**  
  US federal agencies embedding Chief AI Officers signals a strategic prioritization of AI governance, expected to cascade into funded mandates and compliance requirements for contractors and vendors.

- **Hardware Ecosystem Moves:**  
  The newly formed Unified Acceleration Foundation (Google, Intel, Samsung) is a strategic counterbalance to Nvidia’s dominance by developing open AI acceleration software, hinting at shifts in AI infrastructure hardware dependency globally.

- **Ethics and Content Moderation Challenges:**  
  Controversies about biases in AI-generated images (Microsoft and Meta) and artist opposition to AI music synthesis spotlight ongoing societal tensions around AI ethics and intellectual property rights worldwide.

- **Regulatory Pressure on Chinese Apps:**  
  US legislative momentum against TikTok underscores geopolitical risks influencing AI platform availability and user base dynamics, especially regarding data security and ownership concerns.
